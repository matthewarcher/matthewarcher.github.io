I am a physical oceanographer working on NASA's Surface Water and Ocean Topography (SWOT) satellite mission. I’m interested in oceanic variability at the submesoscale (100’s meters to 10’s kilometers), which significantly impacts the large-scale ocean and atmosphere, and ultimately the climate system. As a technologist, I contributed toward the preparation for and evaluation of SWOT, prior to and following its launch in December 2022. Now, I explore science questions that have recently been made possible by SWOT’s unprecedented high-resolution measurements. I moved to JPL in 2018 from the University of New South Wales in Sydney, Australia, where I worked with a variety of observational platforms to investigate the dynamics of the East Australian Current, and how it influences the shelf waters of eastern Australia. Prior to this, I obtained my PhD from the Rosenstiel School of Marine and Atmospheric Science, University of Miami.
